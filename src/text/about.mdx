My first summer internship was as a full stack web developer for a start-up
within Royal York Property Management. We created a web-application with
functionality for tenants, prospective tenants, maintenance contractors, and
leasing agents. We also migrated their CRM data to the app to modernize and
optimize their internal business practices.

I was initially hired as a backend developer, and I began the position with
no prior experience in web development. Instead of letting that hold me back,
I used my lack of experience as encouragement to compensate through hard work
and sheer determination. I voluntarily worked 10+ hour days, earning a
promotion to full stack developer and an extended contract. I led backend
development to stay consistently ahead of schedule, performed a full frontend
overhaul, and learned tons in the process.

This year, I had the opportunity to share my knowledge through my positions
as teaching assistant and as a team lead at QMIND (Queen's AI Hub). My QMIND
team completed a project exploring the use of computer vision for human
attention detection, which we presented at CUCAI 2021. We went on to publish
an award winning paper on our work through the CUCAI Conference Proceedings.

Now I am a Director of Design at QMIND, where I will be guiding several
design teams to explore interesting, AI related problems, and to implement
machine learning based solutions for clients.

I am also currently a summer intern at Mu Lab, where I am designing and
co-authoring a comprehensive library for planning model acquisition - the
"Hugging Face" of automated planning!
